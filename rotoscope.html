What is Rotoscope?

Rotoscoping is an animation technique where animators trace over live-action footage frame by frame. This creates a unique, dreamlike visual style that blends reality with illustration — often used in music videos, experimental films, and fantasy sequences.

The technique was first developed in 1915 by Max Fleischer and has since evolved into both traditional hand-drawn and digital forms. Modern rotoscoping is widely used in VFX, animated features, and motion graphics.
